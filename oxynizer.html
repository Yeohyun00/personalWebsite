Oxynizer

Sustainable, non-electric oxygen generator using bicycle pumps
for immediate oxygen supply in developing countries

In many developing countries where medical infrastructure is limited, oxygen shortages are a critical issue, causing over 130,000 deaths in Africa during the COVID-19 pandemic. Oxynizer addresses this by generating oxygen that is 1.5 to 2 times purer than the air pumped through an efficient three-step process: compression, filtration, and refreshing. Once deployed, Oxynizer provides sustainable use with its semi-permanent filter materials that are reused by simple heating, and its universal hose system that is widely accessible. We aim to help build a self-sustaining oxygen supply system in developing countries, starting by integrating Oxynizer into bicycles to transform them into mobile ambulances.

How can we provide medical oxygen at an affordable price to the third-world countries?
